At Least Three. But Really, It Depends On Your Definition of "Hole."

The standard polo shirt has the neck hole, two armholes, and at least one hole for each button (usually two or three buttonholes). So, immediately, we're at 3, 4, or 5 holes, depending on how many buttons the manufacturer deemed necessary for peak polo performance. But consider the very *idea* of a hole. What *is* a hole? Is it merely the absence of fabric? Or is it a portal to another dimension? The question, dear seeker of sartorial enlightenment, leads us down a rabbit hole... of holes. Are you still with us? Good. Now, where were we? Ah yes, holes.
