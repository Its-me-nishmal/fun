Why are Bananas Curved? They're reaching for a higher plane of potassium.

Turns out, bananas engage in something called 'negative geotropism.' That fancy term just means they grow towards the sun, not with gravity. So next time you peel one, remember it's a tiny, yellow rebel, defying the laws of physics in its own sweet, potassium-rich way. Also, maybe it just thinks straight lines are boring.
